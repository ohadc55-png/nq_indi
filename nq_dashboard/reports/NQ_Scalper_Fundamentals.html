Why So Selective? (~24 Trades Per Year)

The indicator processes 236,135 fifteen-minute bars over 10 years. From this massive dataset, it produces only 267 trades — roughly 2 trades per month. This extreme selectivity is by design: every trade must pass through 7 independent filter layers, each one eliminating the vast majority of potential signals.

The philosophy: It's better to miss 100 mediocre setups than to take 1 bad one. Every filter layer serves a specific purpose: the scoring system demands confluence of trend, volume, structure, momentum, and pattern confirmation. Then session, volatility, day-of-week, and cooldown filters remove anything that doesn't meet the highest quality standard. The result: 53.9% win rate with a 1.87 profit factor — every trade that fires has been validated by 15+ market conditions simultaneously.

The entry threshold adapts based on 4 market confirmations: Supertrend bullish, ADX > 30 + DI+, Volume above average, MTF alignment.

Filter Pipeline (7 Layers)

After scoring, every potential signal must pass through all 7 filters sequentially. Failing any single filter kills the signal. This is why only ~24 trades per year survive.

1. Score >= Effective Threshold Core Gate

Score must beat the dynamic threshold + session penalty + ATR adjustment. In US session with 4 confirmations: score >= 8.0. With 2 confirmations: >= 9.0. This single filter eliminates ~85% of all bars.

2. Bearish Shift Block Safety

After a bearish momentum shift candle (0.5%+ body, red, with volume spike), longs are blocked for 4 bars. No entries allowed until the selling pressure fades. Prevents catching falling knives.

3. EMA Slope Bullish Trend

EMA50 must be rising (current > previous bar). This ensures we only trade with the trend, never against it. Even a perfect score is rejected if the medium-term trend is flat or declining.

4. Day-of-Week Filter Historical

Wednesday and Thursday require score >= 9.0 (extra strict). Historical data showed lower quality on these days. Only 24 Wednesday trades and 17 Thursday trades in 10 years.

5. Europe Session Floor Session

Europe session (02:00-09:30 ET) requires score >= 8.5. Pre-US signals are lower quality. Only 55 Europe trades in 10 years (20.6%).

6. Session Gate Time

No trades during Maintenance (17:00 ET), Closed, or Saturday. Asia session (+2.0 penalty) is effectively blocked since scores rarely reach 9.0+.

7. Cooldown Anti-Overtrade

After each trade, must wait 8 bars (2 hours) OR 0.25% price move. Exception: bullish shift candle or Supertrend flip bypasses cooldown. Prevents re-entering the same move repeatedly.

How a Trade Works

Entry with 2 contracts. Contract 1 exits at TP1 (SL x 1.5). Contract 2 becomes the "runner" with a 3-stage trailing stop. If SL is hit before TP1 — both contracts exit at the stop (FULL_STOP).

3-Stage Trailing Stop (Runner Contract)

Stage 1: Breakeven

Immediately after TP1 hit
Trail = Entry Price
Lock in zero loss on runner
←

Stage 2: Partial

Profit >= SL x 1.5
Trail = Entry + SL x 0.5
Lock in 50% of SL as profit
←

Stage 3: ATR Trail

Profit >= SL x 2.0
Trail = Close - ATR x 2.0
Dynamic, follows Supertrend

Why This System Works

Extreme Selectivity

Only 24 trades per year means every trade has been validated by 15+ market conditions. The 7-layer filter pipeline eliminates 99.9% of potential entries. Quality over quantity.

Runner Alpha

36% of trades reach TRAIL_S3 (ATR trail), generating $287K — 175% of total P&L. The 2-contract split (TP1 + runner) captures both quick profits and big moves. Average TRAIL_S3 profit: $2,990 per trade.

Adaptive Thresholds

The dynamic threshold system adapts to market conditions. When 4 confirmations align: threshold = 8.0 (more trades). When only 1 confirmation: threshold = 9.5 (very strict). Layer 1 ATR adds volatility awareness.

Consistency

All 11 years profitable. OOS performance improved vs IS. 5/6 walk-forward windows pass. Max loss capped at $2,019 per trade. The system doesn't rely on any single year or market condition.
